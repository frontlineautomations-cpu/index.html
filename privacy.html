SMS Consent & Compliance

Important Compliance Information

This page outlines the requirements for SMS consent collection and compliance with telecommunications regulations including TCPA, CTIA guidelines, and A2P 10DLC requirements.

SMS Consent Requirements

Critical Rule

Entering a phone number alone does NOT grant permission to send SMS messages.

SMS consent must be collected separately and explicitly using the following compliant methods:

1. Checkbox Requirement

A checkbox must be required to submit the form. The checkbox cannot be pre-checked and must clearly indicate consent.

☐ I consent to receive SMS messages from Frontline Automations.

2. Separate Consent Types

Consent for non-promotional messages must be separate from consent for marketing content. It must be separate and optional.

  • Transactional: Order confirmations, appointment reminders, account alerts
  • Marketing/Promotional: Sales, offers, newsletters, promotional content

3. Brand Name Consistency

The business name shown must exactly match the Brand Name declared in DBA registration and A2P registration sample messages. Any discrepancy will result in message filtering or blocking.

4. Public Accessibility

Privacy policy and terms must be publicly accessible. They must clearly state that SMS consent is not shared with third parties (except SMS providers necessary for message delivery).

Required SMS Disclosures

All SMS communications and consent forms must clearly state the following:

✓ Who is sending messages

Business name (must match Brand Name registration)

✓ What type of messages will be sent

Describe message content (e.g., "appointment reminders" or "promotional offers")

✓ Message frequency

E.g., "may vary", "up to 4 msgs/month", or specific frequency

✓ Message and data rates may apply

Standard disclosure that carrier charges may apply

✓ How to get HELP

Reply HELP for assistance or provide support contact

✓ How to opt out (STOP)

Reply STOP to unsubscribe at any time

Example Compliant SMS Consent Language

☐ I consent to receive transactional SMS messages from Frontline Automations at the phone number provided. These messages may include appointment reminders, order updates, and account notifications.

☐ I consent to receive promotional SMS messages from Frontline Automations at the phone number provided. These messages may include special offers, news, and marketing content.

Message frequency may vary. Message and data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help or STOP to opt out at any time. Your consent is not shared with third parties except our SMS provider. View our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service for more information.

Marketing vs. Transactional Messages

Transactional Messages

  • Order confirmations
  • Shipping notifications
  • Appointment reminders
  • Password resets
  • Account security alerts
  • Service status updates

Require basic consent but have more flexible rules

Marketing Messages

  • Promotional offers
  • Sales announcements
  • Newsletters
  • Event invitations
  • Product recommendations
  • Surveys and feedback requests

Require express written consent and strict compliance

⚠ Important

Marketing SMS content cannot be mandatory and cannot block form submission. It must be separate and optional. Consent must be unchecked by default.

Opt-Out Requirements

  • STOP keyword: Users can reply STOP to any message to unsubscribe
  • HELP keyword: Users can reply HELP to get assistance and support contact info
  • Alternative methods: Provide email or web-based opt-out options
  • Confirmation: Send a confirmation message after opt-out is processed

Example Auto-Responses:

User sends: STOP

"You have been unsubscribed from Frontline Automations messages. Reply START to resubscribe. For help, reply HELP."

User sends: HELP

"Frontline Automations: For support, email help@frontlineautomations.com. Reply STOP to unsubscribe. Msg&data rates may apply."

A2P 10DLC Registration

Application-to-Person (A2P) 10DLC registration is required for business SMS messaging in the United States. This process includes:

  • Business registration with The Campaign Registry (TCR)
  • Brand verification and vetting
  • Campaign (use case) registration
  • Sample message submission showing exact consent language

Critical: The business name used in messages must exactly match the registered Brand Name and DBA information provided during A2P registration. Mismatches can result in message filtering, blocking, or campaign suspension.
